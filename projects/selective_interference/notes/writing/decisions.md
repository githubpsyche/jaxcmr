# Writing Decisions

## #1: Durability vs. selectivity tension (P1)

- **Date:** 2026-03-02
- **Alternatives considered:**
  - *Responsiveness vs. controllability* — rejected: casts involuntary retrieval as a functional desideratum when it's a consequence of the architecture
  - *Retrieval selectivity over non-selective associations* — rejected: names the answer (retrieval control over competitive associations), not the problem
  - *Preservation vs. relevance* — evolved into durability vs. selectivity through drafting (v1–v9)
- **Decision:** Durability vs. selectivity, with the tension empirically revealed by traumatic experience (not definitionally necessary as in repfr's integration/differentiation). Intrusive memories provide the evidence that earns the tension the opening sentence announces.
- **Rationale:** The C6/C13 structural tension (tension must be genuine, but no mechanism assertion) resolved via rhetoric: adversarial framing ("at the same time"), friction connectives, and the balance metaphor ("outpace"). The tension doesn't need to be logically necessary — it needs to be empirically demonstrated.

## #3: P4/P5 split

- **Date:** 2026-03-03
- **Problem:** Old P4 mixed DRA exposition (S1–S2) and RCT thesis (S3–S5) in one paragraph. Neither got enough space.
- **Decision:** Split into P4 (DRA-only, 5 sentences) + P5 (RCT thesis, 5–6 sentences).
- **Five problems motivating the split:**
  1. P4 mixed DRA exposition and RCT thesis — neither got enough space to convey its explanatory logic
  2. DRA got only 2 sentences — reader can't evaluate the alternative without understanding the dominant account
  3. No reconsolidation coverage — the paper challenges this assumption, so it must be introduced
  4. Thesis narrower than the paper's argument — "without requiring separate traces" named only one of three DRA assumptions challenged
  5. Citation formatting bug — "representations. [@brewin1996; @brewin2014]." — period before citation
- **New constraints:** P4-C1 through P4-C5, P5-C1 through P5-C4, A6 (standalone self-sufficiency).

## #5: P5 closing motivation sentence (S6 added)

- **Date:** 2026-03-03

- **Decision:** Added P5-C5 (closing motivation) and S6: "By grounding selective interference in general principles of temporal context and retrieval competition, the account connects a clinical phenomenon to the broader episodic memory literature and generates predictions that diverge from the dual-representation account and can sharpen intervention designs."
